Trump's Impeachment: Constitutional Process or Political Theater?

The impeachment proceedings against former President Trump have sparked intense debate about the constitutional framework for holding presidents accountable. Supporters argue that impeachment is a necessary check on executive power and that no one is above the law, while critics contend that the process has become overly politicized and undermines democratic institutions.

Key questions remain: Was the impeachment process conducted fairly and according to constitutional principles? How should we balance accountability with the stability of democratic governance?
